Types of Calligraphy

Gothic

Sharp, dramatic lines with medieval aesthetic from 12th-century Europe.

Copperplate

Elegant, flowing curves using a pointed nib; 18th-century origin.

Arabic

Ornamental and spiritual, central to Islamic art.

Chinese

One of the oldest forms; emphasizes brush control and rhythm.

Modern

Blends cursive handwriting with artistic flair and freedom.

Brush Script

Uses expressive strokes with a brush; popular in East Asia.

Italic

A Renaissance-era style known for its slightly slanted and smooth strokes.

Faux Calligraphy

This friendly technique uses regular pens to mimic the look of calligraphy.

Urdu Calligraphy

A fusion of Nastaliq and artistic flair, Urdu calligraphy captures poetic expression and visual grace.
